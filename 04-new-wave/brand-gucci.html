The House

A Century of Florence,
Worn on the Face

Founded in Florence in 1921 by Guccio Gucci, the House began as a maker of fine leather goods and equestrian equipment — a heritage still visible in every green-red-green Web stripe and horsebit detail. Under a century of Italian craftsmanship, Gucci grew from a Florentine workshop into fashion's most recognisable name, its interlocking GG monogram a signature since 1933.

Gucci eyewear, crafted in Italy by Kering Eyewear, carries these codes onto the face: bold acetates, gold-tone hardware, the GG mark at the temple, and silhouettes that move between quiet elegance and unapologetic maximalism. At Broadway Opticians, we are proud to bring authentic Gucci eyewear to Mussoorie.

Notes of the House

  • Founded by Guccio Gucci in Florence, Italy, where the House remains headquartered to this day.
  • The interlocking GG monogram was designed by Aldo Gucci, from his father Guccio Gucci's initials.
  • The horsebit — the double-ring-and-bar hardware — dates to the Gucci loafer of 1953 and recurs on eyewear temples.
  • The green-red-green Web stripe derives from equestrian saddle girth straps, a mark of the House's saddlery origins.
  • Part of the Kering group since 1999; Gucci eyewear is designed and produced by Kering Eyewear, manufactured in Italy.
